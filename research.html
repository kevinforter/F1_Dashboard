Project Evolution

This project documents the journey from a rough initial concept to a data-driven F1 dashboard. Below is the history of our design process, including early sketches, initial implementations, and the critical feedback that shaped the final product.

Phase 1: The Concept

Our initial brainstorming focused on mapping race locations and visualizing simple linear progressions of driver standings.

Phase 2: First Implementation

We built a functioning prototype ("First Draft") attempting to combine a world map with a line chart. However, this version revealed significant design flaws regarding information density and clarity.

Critical Feedback Loop

1. Map Information & Legend "World map is not very informative → what do the different colors mean? Add a legend."
2. Chart Interpolation Issues "The line chart needs to be adjusted: it’s problematic because it’s not data-driven. Polynomial interpolation leads to this 'O'-shape, adding things that don’t actually exist. If kept, use straight lines instead of rounded ones."
3. Clarity of Purpose "The goal of the dashboard needs to be presented more clearly: what is the dashboard supposed to say? Best driver? It needs a sharper focus."
4. Depth of Analytics "Take a step further into analytics: possibly compare drivers or teams over the years, rather than just showing raw results."

Phase 3: Refinement & Pivot

Based on the feedback, we fundamentally restructured the dashboard to focus on Comparative Analytics rather than just descriptive data.

  • Narrowing the Scope: We followed the instructor's advice to include "only drivers or constructors" to reduce noise, focusing on the human element of the sport.
  • Modern Era Dataset (2010+): We restricted the dataset to 2010 onwards. This was a strategic choice due to the major points system overhaul (25 points for a win), ensuring that our analytical comparisons remain statistically valid without needing complex normalization algorithms.
  • Visual Integrity: We abandoned polynomial interpolation for the charts, switching to straight lines to truthfully represent the discrete nature of race-by-race point accumulation.
