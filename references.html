References & Data

The foundation of this dashboard relies on accurate historical data and open-source research. Below are the primary sources used for data ingestion and domain research.

Data Sources

The raw CSV files processing the dashboard's analytics are sourced from:

  • Kaggle: Formula 1 World Championship (1950 - 2024)

    Maintained by Rohan Rao. This dataset provides the comprehensive history of F1, from which we filter for the modern era (2010-Present).

  • Ergast Developer API

    The original source for the CSV compilations, providing detailed lap times, pit stops, and qualifying data.

  • TopoJSON: World Atlas

    Source map data (countries-110m.json) used for rendering the geographical visualizations (World Atlas v2).

Contextual information regarding the 2024 season and the evolution of regulations was cross-referenced with:

Research Findings

Our interactive analysis of the dataset revealed several key insights regarding the sport's dynamics:

  • 1. The "Pole Position" Advantage

    Analysis of results.csv highlights a strong correlation between starting on the front row (Grid 1-2) and race wins. This finding validates our design choice to include the "Performance Matrix", which explicitly visualizes the delta between Qualifying (Grid) and Race (Position Order) results.

  • 2. Circuit-Dependent Overtaking

    By filtering `results.csv` data against specific IDs in circuits.csv, we observed that street circuits (e.g., Monaco) show significantly lower variance between start and finish positions compared to purpose-built tracks (e.g., Spa, Silverstone). This confirms the "processional" nature often criticized by fans for specific tracks.

  • 3. Consistency vs. Peak Performance

    Longitudinal analysis of the `driver_standings.csv` reveals that championships in the hybrid era (2014+) are won by consistency (high average finish) rather than just peak win rate. Drivers with fewer DNF (Did Not Finish) statuses often outperform faster but less reliable rivals over a full season.
