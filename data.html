Data Source

The data for this project is sourced from the comprehensive Formula 1 World Championship (1950 - 2025) dataset on Kaggle (maintained by Rohan Rao).


Scope & Filtering

While the original dataset covers the history of F1 from 1950, this dashboard focuses exclusively on the modern era: 2010 to Present.

Why 2010?

In 2010, Formula 1 introduced a major overhaul of the points system (25-18-15-12-10-8-6-4-2-1), drastically changing how championships were decided compared to previous eras (where wins were worth only 10 or 9 points). Focusing on this period ensures consistent statistical comparison and reflects the dynamics of modern racing.

Used Datasets

The project utilizes the following CSV files from the dataset to generate its visualizations:

races.csv

Contains the schedule and information for every Grand Prix.

AttributeTypeDescription
raceIdIntegerUnique identifier for the race
yearIntegerSeason year (e.g., 2023)
roundIntegerSequential round number in the season
circuitIdIntegerForeign key links to circuits.csv
nameStringOfficial name of the Grand Prix
dateDateDate of the race

results.csv

The core dataset containing race results for every driver in every race.

AttributeTypeDescription
resultIdIntegerUnique identifier for the result
raceIdIntegerLinks to the specific race
driverIdIntegerLinks to the specific driver
gridIntegerStarting grid position
positionOrderIntegerFinal finishing rank (numeric)
pointsFloatPoints scored in the race
statusIdIntegerFinishing status (Finished, Collision, etc.)

drivers.csv

Information about the drivers taking part in the championships.

AttributeTypeDescription
driverIdIntegerUnique identifier for the driver
codeString3-letter driver code (e.g., HAM, VER)
forenameStringDriver's first name
surnameStringDriver's last name
nationalityStringDriver's nationality

circuits.csv

Geographical data for all F1 tracks.

AttributeTypeDescription
circuitIdIntegerUnique identifier for the circuit
nameStringName of the circuit
locationStringCity location
countryStringCountry location
lat/lngFloatGeographical coordinates

driver_standings.csv

Cumulative championship standings after each race.

AttributeTypeDescription
raceIdIntegerThe race at which standings are calculated
driverIdIntegerThe driver
pointsFloatCumulative total points
positionIntegerRank in the championship
winsIntegerCount of wins so far in season

Design Choices

Foundations of Visual Design

We utilized a dark theme to match the premium, technological aesthetic of Formula 1. The color palette relies on high contrast (White on Dark Grey) for readability, with F1 Red used sparingly to guide attention to interactive elements and key headings.

Data-Ink Ratio

The dashboard minimizes non-data ink. Grid lines are faint or removed. The heatmap uses color intensity to convey density without needing explicit numbers in every cell (details on demand).

Human Perception

We use position (Bar Chart) and Color Saturation (Heatmap) which are strong pre-attentive attributes. The "Grid Position" (Cause) is placed on the X-axis and "Result" (Effect) on the Y-axis, following standard convention.
